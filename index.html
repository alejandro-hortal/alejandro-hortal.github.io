About

Scholarship at the intersection of ethics, rationality & governance

My research focuses on the philosophical foundations of government and public policy, examining how human rationality, moral agency, and responsibility are shaped by institutions, technologies, and cultural contexts. Working at the intersection of political philosophy, philosophy of science, and the behavioral and social sciences, I explore how contemporary governance influences citizens' capacity for judgment, autonomy, and meaningful participation in democratic public life.

A central strand of my work analyzes bounded and ecological rationality, drawing on the legacy of Herbert Simon. Concepts such as heuristics, biases, nudges, and choice architecture function in my research not merely as technical policy tools, but as philosophical lenses through which to assess the ethical limits of political influence and the conditions under which public policies can support responsible agency rather than passive compliance.

My research addresses substantive political challenges — public health governance, gender-based violence, corruption, and sustainability — while critically examining the epistemological assumptions behind policy design. Drawing on Aristotelian virtue ethics, I argue that effective governance must attend not only to outcomes and compliance, but to the cultivation of stable dispositions that support responsible agency and democratic participation.

I hold dual US-Spanish citizenship and maintain active research collaborations across Europe, Mexico, and Colombia. I serve as Guest Editor of a Behavioural Public Policy special issue, Advisory Board member of the IBPPA, and Board Director of the Scientific Association of Behavioral Economics (Spain & Latin America).

Research Agenda

Current Projects & Research Interests

Phronetic Framework for Behavioral Public Policy

Developing bounded phronesis and the p-perspective as normative criteria for evaluating behavioral interventions. Introduces multi-rationality (six forms) as an alternative to Kahneman's dual-system model. Integrates Aristotelian virtue ethics with contemporary behavioral science.

Ethics & Epistemology of Nudge Theory

Critical examination of the conceptual and normative foundations of nudge-based behavioral interventions, including the limits of evidence-based policymaking, RCTs across diverse cultural settings, and the risks of epistemic dilution under nudge saturation.

Behavioral Approaches to Corruption & Gender Violence

Normative and empirical analysis of how choice architecture and virtue nudges can address systemic social problems including corruption in Latin America and gender-based violence, while respecting autonomy and dignity. 🏆 Best Research Project Prize, SCEC (2024).

Bounded Rationality & Herbert Simon's Legacy

Tracing the genealogy of bounded rationality from Simon through ecological rationality (Gigerenzer) to contemporary BPP, examining the philosophical implications of heuristics, satisficing, and procedural rationality for political theory and democratic governance.

Deaths of Despair & Hopelessness

Examining how structural hopelessness drives deaths of despair and how behavioral interventions can help while respecting autonomy. Joint work with Dr. Jack Kwong (Appalachian State). Under review at Journal of Social Philosophy and Public Health Ethics.

Virtue Nudges & Civic Character Formation

Investigating how choice architecture can be designed to cultivate civic virtues and practical wisdom, not merely compliant behavior. Connects to extended cognition, digital environments, and sustainability policy. Collaboration with Dr. Stefano Calboli (Sant'Anna).
